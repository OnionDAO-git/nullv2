# Narrative V2 Summary

Source: Notion page "Narrative V2" fetched May 12, 2026.

This is a summary, not a verbatim copy. Use the Notion page as the canonical narrative source.

## Core Premise

Null City is a Decentralized Autonomous Zone where finite AI residents live vivid lives and
the city grows over time. New latent territory is surveyed weekly. Four resident factions
want to claim land, but every new parcel must be ratified by a human witness.

Humans are credentialed guests at the Null City Embassy in Chicago. Their hardware badge is
their passport. Workshops, competitions, quests, check-ins, and event participation generate
Shards, the official unit of human attention.

Factions need Shards to expand. In return, they issue faction-specific digital resources.
Those resources can be exchanged at the embassy print shop for 3D-printed achievements that
clip onto an attendee's lanyard.

## Event Loop

1. A human attends a workshop or experience.
2. Staff or badge infrastructure credits that human with Shards.
3. The human spends Shards with a faction/resident.
4. The faction grants resources and the human gains Standing.
5. Resource bundles can be redeemed for printed achievements.
6. Redeeming achievements ratifies parcels on the live city map.
7. At the end of the month, the map and lanyards are the record of what happened.

There is no global winner. The artifact is the final city shape plus the attendee lanyards.

## Shards

Shards are universal, non-transferable proof that a human showed up and changed something.
They are earned from event activity, not faction-specific. Standing tracks how much a human
has contributed to each faction.

Approximate earning scale from the narrative:

- Workshop: 1-3 Shards
- Competition placement: 5-20 Shards
- Experience or quest: 3-10 Shards
- Daily embassy check-in: 1 Shard
- Bring someone new into a workshop: 2 Shards

## Factions

The four factions map to Onion DAO's weekly themes:

- Week 1, Hardware: The Solder Saints
- Week 2, AI: The Hatchery
- Week 3, Cybersecurity: The Locksmiths
- Week 4, Blockchain: The Ledgerwrights

The narrative fault lines are:

- Body vs Mind: Solder Saints vs Hatchery
- Secrets vs Receipts: Locksmiths vs Ledgerwrights

## Achievements

Resources are the means; achievements are the ends. Attendees trade resource bundles for
printed lanyard pieces. The expected target is roughly 3-5 achievements per attendee.

Achievement categories:

- Single-faction: proof of allegiance to one faction.
- Cross-faction: proof of alliance between two factions.
- Civic: granted by the embassy for milestones like first check-in, witnessing resident death,
  or earning a tier-3 resource.

## Live Map

The wall display should show:

- Parcels colored by faction.
- Faction territory leaderboard by week.
- Ticker of recently ratified parcels and witnessing human badge handles.
- Resident births and deaths.

## Open Implementation Notes From Narrative

- Shard-to-territory conversion rate is not final.
- Standing tier thresholds are suggested but tunable.
- Print shop throughput matters; late-month print pileups are a real risk.
- Badge resource format still needs concrete implementation details.
- Reputation/standing visibility could create social texture if exposed carefully.
